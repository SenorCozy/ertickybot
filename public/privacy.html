Privacy Policy

Elden Ring Ticket Bot · Last updated 16 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information the Elden Ring Ticket Bot ("the Bot", "we", "us") collects when you use it inside our Discord community, how that information is used, and the choices you have. By using the Bot's features you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information we collect

The Bot only processes data needed to run a support-ticket system:

  • Discord identifiers. Your Discord user ID and username, so tickets and transcripts can be attributed to the right person.
  • Ticket content. When a support ticket is closed, the messages sent in that ticket channel — including their text, author, and timestamps — are saved as a transcript for staff record-keeping.
  • Ticket metadata. The platform you select (PlayStation, PC, Xbox), which helper claimed the ticket, open/close times, ticket status, and the stated closure reason.
  • Limited message reading. To power the "thank a helper" reputation prompt, the Bot reads messages in the server to detect thank-you phrases that mention another member. This check runs in memory at the moment a message is sent and is not stored separately.
  • Aggregate statistics. Counts such as the number of unique ticket users and tickets opened per platform, used only for community statistics.
  • Staff dashboard sign-in. If you are a moderator and log in to the Bot's web dashboard, we use Discord's OAuth to confirm your identity, server membership, and roles (scopes: identify, guilds, guilds.members.read). A session is kept for up to 7 days so you don't have to sign in repeatedly.

2. How we use your information

  • To create, route, manage, and close support tickets.
  • To notify platform helpers when a relevant ticket is opened.
  • To keep transcripts of closed tickets for moderation and quality purposes.
  • To verify that only authorized moderators can use administrative commands and the web dashboard.
  • To display community statistics.

3. How message content is handled

We are explicit about this because the Bot requires Discord's Message Content permission:

  • Message content is read only for the two purposes above: detecting thank-you reputation phrases, and building a transcript when a ticket is closed.
  • Message content is never sent to any third party and is never used to train machine-learning or AI models.
  • The Bot does not track presence, online status, or member activity.

4. Data storage and security

  • Data is stored in a private database on a server controlled by the Bot's operator.
  • Transcripts and the web dashboard are restricted to server moderators. Access requires signing in with Discord and passing a moderator-role check, so a transcript link shared in a staff channel cannot be opened by anyone who is not an authenticated moderator.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties.

5. Data retention

Ticket records and transcripts are retained for as long as they remain useful for moderation and record-keeping. You may ask us to delete data relating to you, and we will do so unless we are required to keep it for a legitimate moderation reason.

6. Your rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of the personal data the Bot holds about you. To make a request, contact the community's moderation team or email jpbackup89@gmail.com.

7. Children's privacy

Discord requires all users to be at least 13 years old (or older where local law sets a higher age). The Bot is not intended for anyone below that age, and we do not knowingly collect their data.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.
